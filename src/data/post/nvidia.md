### 1. Investment Summary and "Outperform" Thesis

NVIDIA has successfully completed its evolution from a silicon component vendor to the premier architect of the "AI Factory" era. We reiterate our **"Outperform"** rating and **Zacks Rank #1 (Strong Buy)** status. This conviction is driven by a fundamental shift in the global compute paradigm: the transition from "retrieval-based" computing—where data is simply stored and fetched—to "generative" computing, where infrastructure is designed to produce intelligence as a commodity.

NVIDIA is the sole provider of a full-stack ecosystem (GPU, Networking, CUDA) that addresses the three current platform shifts: the move from general-purpose to accelerated computing necessitated by the slowing of Moore’s Law, the replacement of classical machine learning with generative AI, and the rise of Agentic/Physical AI. This transition creates a deflationary cost environment for hyperscalers; by optimizing "performance per watt" and driving down "cost per token," NVIDIA delivers a Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) that competitors using fragmented ASIC solutions cannot match.

### 3. The Blackwell Transition and the $500 Billion Revenue Roadmap

The transition to the Blackwell architecture (GB200/GB300) serves as the bridge to the next generation of gigawatt-scale AI factories. Management has provided visibility into **$500 billion in revenue for Blackwell and Rubin** from the start of CY2025 through the end of CY2026.

Compute demand is sustained by three scaling laws that require exponential increases in compute:

1. **Pre-training:** Larger foundation models require exponentially more data/compute to achieve intelligence breakthroughs.
2. **Post-training:** New reasoning algorithms allow models to "think" step-by-step, requiring massive compute after initial training.
3. **Inference:** As AI moves to real-time applications, the volume of computation necessary for response generation is reaching a tipping point.

**Technical Superiority:** In recent MLPerf training results, Blackwell Ultra delivered **5x faster time-to-train** than Hopper. Furthermore, on the Semi-Analysis "Inference Max" benchmark, Blackwell achieved the lowest TCO in the industry. For reasoning models like DeepSeek R1, Blackwell delivered **10x higher performance per watt** and **10x lower cost per token** compared to the H200.

**The "So What?" Layer:** The "Blackwell crossover"—where GB300 demand has already exceeded GB200—highlights NVIDIA's execution speed. This seamless transition protects NVIDIA’s competitive moat by ensuring its latest, most efficient architecture is the "universal" choice for every frontier model, preventing ASICs from gaining a foothold during architectural shifts.
